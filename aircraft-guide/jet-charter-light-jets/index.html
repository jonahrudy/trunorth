Frequently Asked Questions About Light Jet Charter

What's the difference between light jets and turboprops?

Light jets are faster and have longer range than turboprops, making them ideal for cross-country travel and trips over 600 miles. They cruise at higher altitudes (41,000-45,000 feet) for smoother flights above weather. Light jets are perfect for coast-to-coast business travel, while turboprops excel at shorter regional trips.

How many passengers can a light jet accommodate?

Most light jets comfortably seat 4-7 passengers with enclosed lavatories and adequate luggage capacity. They offer quieter cabins than turboprops while remaining cost-effective for small groups.

What's the typical range of a light jet?

Light jets typically have a range of 1,500-2,000 nautical miles, allowing non-stop flights like Los Angeles to Dallas, San Francisco to Chicago, or coast-to-coast travel with one fuel stop. This makes them ideal for regional or multi-leg business trips.

Can light jets fly into smaller airports?

Yes! Light jets can access countless airports including executive FBOs and regional airports that commercial airlines don't serve. While they require longer runways than turboprops, they still offer exceptional flexibility and performance for business travel.

How far in advance should I book a light jet charter?

While we can accommodate same-day requests when available, we recommend booking three to eight weeks from departure for optimal aircraft selection, scheduling flexibility, and competitive pricing.

What amenities are included in light jets?

Light jets feature comfortable cabins, enclosed lavatories, refreshment centers, and Wi-Fi capability. Many also include work tables for productivity during flight.
