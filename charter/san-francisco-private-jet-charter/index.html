Frequently Asked Questions

What airport do private jets use in San Francisco?

The Bay Area has four active private jet airports:

  • SFO (San Francisco International): Handles the widest aircraft range including heavy jets. Primary departure for Hawaii and international routes. FBOs: Signature Flight Support, Atlantic Aviation.
  • OAK (Oakland International): Less congestion than SFO, often more cost-effective for domestic routes. Preferred for East Bay clients.
  • SJC (San José International): Best for South Bay and Silicon Valley departures — Cupertino, San José, and nearby tech campuses.
  • PAO (Palo Alto Airport): Fastest FBO experience in the region. No TSA, direct ramp access. Ideal for turboprops and light jets. Preferred for Sand Hill Road and downtown Palo Alto.
How much does a private jet from San Francisco to Las Vegas cost?

Charter from the Bay Area to Las Vegas typically ranges by aircraft type:

  • Light Jet (most popular): $8,000–$13,000 one-way — covers the route in ~55 minutes
  • Midsize Jet: $13,000–$18,000 one-way — more cabin space for larger groups
  • Turboprop: $6,000–$9,000 one-way — slower but cost-effective for small groups

Pricing increases during peak demand weekends — major Las Vegas events, New Year's, Super Bowl weekend. Contact TruNorth for a same-day quote on your specific dates.

Can I fly privately from Oakland or San José instead of SFO?

Yes — both OAK and SJC are fully operational private charter airports with FBO facilities, fuel, and ground handling.

  • Oakland (OAK): Often less congested than SFO and more cost-effective for domestic routes. Strong choice for East Bay clients.
  • San José (SJC): Best option for South Bay — closest to Cupertino, San José, and major tech campuses.
  • Palo Alto (PAO): Fastest total departure experience. No commercial airline overhead, direct ramp access. Handles turboprops and light jets.
What is the best jet for flying from San Francisco to Los Angeles?

A light jet is the ideal choice for SFO to LA — the flight is approximately 55 minutes and light jet models (Phenom 300, Citation CJ4) offer an excellent experience at a lower cost than midsize.

Recommended LA routings from the Bay Area:

  • SFO → Van Nuys (VNY): Best for West LA, Beverly Hills, and the San Fernando Valley — avoids LAX entirely
  • SFO → Burbank (BUR): Best for Burbank, Glendale, Studio City, and Hollywood
  • SFO → Santa Monica (SMO): Westside LA — convenient for Venice, Santa Monica, and Malibu

Midsize jets work well when you're bringing a full group of 7–9 or want a stand-up cabin for the short flight.

How far in advance should I book a charter flight from the Bay Area?

Peak periods — book 2–4 weeks ahead:

  • Ski season to Aspen and Sun Valley (January through March)
  • Major Las Vegas events and holiday weekends
  • Summer Hawaii departures (June through August)
  • New Year's, Thanksgiving, and July 4th

Standard travel — 48–72 hours is typically sufficient for most Bay Area routes including Las Vegas, LA, and Scottsdale at competitive pricing.

Same-day and last-minute: Available on select routes and aircraft types, but aircraft selection is limited and pricing is higher. PAO departures often have better last-minute availability than SFO.

Does TruNorth Jets serve Palo Alto Airport (PAO)?

Yes. PAO is a preferred departure point for Silicon Valley clients and we coordinate charters from Palo Alto regularly.

Why PAO works well for Bay Area private charter:

  • No TSA screening or commercial airline congestion
  • Direct ramp access — fastest gate-to-wheels-up time in the region
  • Significantly closer to Sand Hill Road, downtown Palo Alto, and major tech campuses than SFO
  • Best for turboprops and light jets — common aircraft types for Las Vegas, LA, and Reno/Tahoe runs

For heavier aircraft or Hawaii-bound flights, SFO is the required departure point. We advise on the right airport for your aircraft type when quoting.

Can I bring my pet on a private charter from San Francisco?

Yes. Private charter is pet-friendly by default — dogs and cats travel in the cabin with you, no carrier required for the flight itself. Unlike commercial airlines, there are no breed restrictions or size limits on most charter aircraft.

Let us know when requesting a quote so we can confirm with the operator. There are typically no additional pet fees on charter flights.

What is included in private jet charter pricing from San Francisco?

Included in your charter quote:

  • Aircraft and crew
  • Fuel and standard routing
  • Standard FBO fees at Bay Area airports
  • Flight planning and coordination

Available upon request (not included by default):

  • Catering and specialty beverages
  • Ground transportation arrangements
  • Overnight crew expenses on multi-day trips
  • International fees (customs, overflight permits, handling) for Mexico and Hawaii flights

TruNorth Jets provides all-inclusive quotes so you know the exact cost before confirming. No surprise charges at departure.
